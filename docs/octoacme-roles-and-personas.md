# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Collaboration Points
- Works with QA Lead on test strategies and acceptance
- Engages with UX Designer on implementation of design specs
- Partners with Technical Writer on API and code documentation

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Collaboration Points
- Works closely with Business Analyst to translate requirements
- Partners with UX Designer on user experience priorities
- Aligns with QA Lead on acceptance criteria and test plans

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Collaboration Points
- Works with Release Manager to coordinate deployment schedules
- Partners with Business Analyst on requirements and scope
- Aligns with all team members on timelines and dependencies

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own the testing strategy, quality practices, and acceptance validation for projects. They work cross-functionally with developers, product managers, and release managers to ensure features meet quality standards before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain test strategies (unit, integration, end-to-end, security)
- Create and maintain test plans aligned with acceptance criteria
- Coordinate QA activities with the delivery team
- Identify and prioritize quality risks and defects
- Validate acceptance criteria are met before release
- Mentor and guide QA engineers and test automation efforts
- Establish quality metrics and dashboards

### Goals
- Ensure features meet quality standards and acceptance criteria
- Reduce post-release defects and customer impact
- Build confidence in releases through comprehensive testing
- Maintain clear, actionable defect reporting

### Typical Communication
- QA planning meetings during sprint planning
- Defect reports and status updates in daily standups
- Test plan reviews with developers and product managers
- Quality readiness assessments before releases

### Collaboration Points
- Partners with Developers on test automation and continuous integration
- Aligns with Product Manager on acceptance criteria and success metrics
- Works with Release Manager on pre-release testing and sign-offs
- Consults with UX Designer on usability testing and scenarios

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers own user experience and design deliverables. They collaborate with product managers and developers to ensure solutions are usable, accessible, and aligned with customer needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specs
- Define interaction patterns and user flows
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design standards
- Participate in design reviews and feedback sessions
- Document design decisions and rationale
- Work with developers to ensure design fidelity in implementation

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction and improve customer satisfaction
- Ensure design consistency across features and platforms
- Validate design decisions through user feedback

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with product managers and developers
- User research findings and insights
- Design specification documents and prototypes
- Feedback on implemented features

### Collaboration Points
- Partners with Product Manager to validate user needs and prioritize features
- Works with Developers to ensure implementation matches design specs
- Collaborates with Business Analyst to understand business context
- Consults with Technical Writer on help documentation and UI copy

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts translate business needs and stakeholder requirements into clear, actionable specifications. They bridge the gap between business stakeholders and delivery teams, ensuring solutions address the underlying business problem.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create requirement specifications and acceptance criteria
- Act as liaison between stakeholders and delivery teams
- Validate that solutions meet business objectives
- Document business decisions and rationale
- Support change management and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Ensure alignment between business needs and solution delivery
- Reduce misalignment and scope creep
- Improve stakeholder understanding and buy-in
- Enable data-driven decision-making

### Typical Communication
- Requirements gathering meetings with stakeholders
- Business requirement documents and specifications
- Impact analysis and trade-off assessments
- Stakeholder updates and change notices

### Collaboration Points
- Works closely with Product Manager on requirement prioritization
- Partners with Project Manager on scope and timeline trade-offs
- Collaborates with QA Lead to define acceptance criteria
- Engages with Developers on technical feasibility and constraints

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers plan and coordinate releases, manage deployment processes, and ensure smooth cutover communications. They work with product, engineering, QA, and operations teams to execute releases with minimal risk and disruption.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain release plans and schedules
- Coordinate with development, QA, and operations teams
- Manage release notes and cutover communications
- Coordinate pre-release testing and sign-offs
- Manage deployment to staging and production environments
- Prepare and execute rollback plans if needed
- Document post-release verifications and metrics
- Communicate release status to stakeholders

### Goals
- Execute releases with minimal risk and downtime
- Ensure clear communication across all stakeholders
- Enable fast, confident deployments
- Maintain detailed release records for compliance and learning

### Typical Communication
- Release schedules and cutover plans
- Deployment readiness checklists and approvals
- Release notes and communication templates
- Post-deployment verification reports

### Collaboration Points
- Works with Project Manager on release timing and dependencies
- Coordinates with QA Lead on pre-release testing and sign-offs
- Partners with Developers on deployment readiness and rollback procedures
- Communicates with Technical Writer on release announcements and documentation updates

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain clear, accurate documentation for users and internal teams. They ensure processes, APIs, and features are well-documented and accessible to all audiences.

### Responsibilities
- Document user-facing features and help content
- Create and maintain API documentation
- Document internal processes and runbooks
- Maintain style guides and documentation standards
- Collaborate with teams to ensure accuracy and completeness
- Update documentation based on feature changes and feedback
- Create training materials and onboarding content

### Goals
- Enable users and teams to self-serve with clear documentation
- Reduce support burden through comprehensive help content
- Maintain consistency and accessibility across all documentation
- Support onboarding and knowledge sharing

### Typical Communication
- Documentation updates tied to feature releases
- Style guide and template guidance for the team
- Feedback on API and feature clarity
- Content reviews with subject-matter experts

### Collaboration Points
- Works with Developers on API and code documentation
- Collaborates with UX Designer on UI copy and help content
- Partners with Product Manager on feature documentation and release notes
- Engages with QA Lead on acceptance criteria documentation
